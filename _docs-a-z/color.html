Background-color Classes

In the NYC Core Framework white is treated as a color and it serves as your default theme color. Throughout our templates and examples, you may have noticed the <body> tag has a .theme-white class applied (see Figure 1 below).

In the Framework, each theme color (e.g. Primary) is assigned a designated text color, link color, and border color.

This designation occurs within our color system's Sass partial. Notice the Primary theme color declarations are highlighted in Figure 2 below.

When a background-color class is applied to an element, its text, borders, and links (only those inside <p> tags) automatically inherit their appropriate predesignated color.

Inheritance and Selectivity

Apply a background-color class to any element along the DOM (Document Object Model) and every child element within inherits their appropriate colors until a new class is specified.

In other words, background-color classes are infinitely nestable. Let's look at this concept of nesting put in practice.
