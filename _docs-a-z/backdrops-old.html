Backdrops are useful for placing a translucent image behind custom content. Backdrops offer two distinct variations. Image proportional backdrops scale and stack responsively. Fixed-height backdrops remain at a fixed height across breakpoints. When needed, they adjust to the height of their inner content.

Image Proportional

The backdrop component requires a parent .backdrop selector, and two child selectors. The .backdrop__image child selector contains the feature image. Its sibling, the .backdrop__cover selector, contains the contextual content for the backdrop.

Within image proportional backdrops, the .backdrop__cover selector is absolutely positioned over .backdrop__image.

Breakpoint Modifiers

When using image size proportional backdrops for featured content (like the full-width example below) the content may become too tall for the image on small viewports.

Include a stack breakpoint modifier (e.g. .backdrop--stack--md) to the .backdrop selector and the backdrop's image is placed above the content vertically until the viewport reaches the desired breakpoint specified in the modifier.
