Fair and equitable experiences for people with all abilities.

Everyone deserves equal opportunities to engage with digital content online. The NYC Core Framework is designed to demystify web accessibility—and help you integrate accessible best practices into your everyday workflow. Web accessibility is not only the right thing to do—it is now becoming the law.

In this section, learn about the high-level accessibility features built into the NYC Core Framework, and circumstances to look out for when developing your custom templates.

Valid HTML

The first and easiest step towards better accessibility is to create valid HTML, which better provides a consistent experience across all browsers and assistive technologies.

Our prebuilt templates have been validated through the W3C Nu Html Checker. It is a good idea to periodically check your work here, too at different stages of development.

Color Contrast

With respect to accessibility, color contrast refers the difference in color between text and background color or image. When applying your own colors to our color system, check the color-contrast ratio to ensure readability. To be compliant with WCAG Level AA, aim for a contrast ratio of 4.5:1 or higher.

Learn how our color system helps you keep your accessible colors consistent throughout your layouts.

Landmarks

Landmarks refer to the use of regions and roles throughout an HTML document. Common regions are <header>, <main>, and <footer>. Common roles are role="banner", role="list", and role="button".

Landmarks allow screen readers quick access to regions helps communicate important information about the page layout. As an example, adding <role="list"> to a <ul> provides the number of list items to the screen reader.
